# Research Methodology
**CS663: Human Factors & Usability Metrics · Pace University · Spring 2026**

---

## Research Objectives

### Primary
- Identify what motivates product-level decisions: price, brand loyalty, unit value, ratings, perceived quality
- Understand key factors driving store selection: proximity, familiarity, reputation, availability, delivery fees

### Secondary
- Evaluate ease and efficiency of browsing, search, and filtering
- Identify where the interface supports or creates friction in the path to purchase

---

## Methodology

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| Method | 1:1 remote moderated usability testing + semi-structured interview |
| Protocol | Think-aloud, reminding participants where needed |
| Platform | Zoom, screen-sharing enabled |
| Device | Participant's own computer — Instacart desktop website |
| Environment | Participant's natural environment (reduces artificial behaviour) |
| Recording | Video + audio, post-study transcription and coding |
| Facilitation | 1 facilitator + 1 note-taker per session |

## Analysis Approach

Post-session analysis used thematic coding across 8 overarching themes:

1. Familiarity & Routine (Mental Model)
2. Search Dependency (Usability)
3. Category Mental Models (Mental Model + Usability)
4. Filter Failures (Usability)
5. Product Card Information Gaps (Usability)
6. Budget as Total Cost (Mental Model)
7. Cultural & Specialty Food Needs (Human-Centred)
8. Visual Overwhelm (Usability)

Each finding was tagged by sentiment (positive / negative / neutral) and type (Mental Model / Usability / Navigation / General).

---

## Facilitator Notes

- Neutral tone maintained throughout
- Neutral probes used: *"What makes you say that?"* / *"What were you expecting?"*
- Silence allowed — participants given time to think before intervention
- Task 3 designed specifically to expose browsing behaviour by restricting search access
